# Landing Page Hot Tips #01-25

## #01 - Utilize Customer Testimonials
Highlight features and answer doubts through testimonials.

**Bad testimonial:** "Great product, highly recommend!" - Generic, no value

**Good testimonial:** "The build quality exceeded expectations and arrived perfectly packaged despite international shipping." - Highlights feature (build quality) + answers doubt (shipping damage)

**Action:** Review all testimonials, keep only those adding specific value.

**Resources:**
- How to request testimonials: Ask customers about specific features and concerns
- Testimonial examples: onepagelove.com testimonial collection

---

## #02 - Showcase Testimonials from Similar Demographics
Match testimonial sources to your target audience.

Selling enterprise software? Feature testimonials from enterprise customers with their title and company.

**Example:** "Dave Lewis, VP Customer Relations at Starbucks" carries more weight for enterprise buyers than "Dave L."

**Resource:** Avataaars - online avatar generator for when customer imagery unavailable

---

## #03 - Fewer Images, Better Images
Good imagery builds trust. Trust is the foundation for conversions.

**Action:** Invest in professional photoshoot of team, product, or service. ROI is virtually guaranteed.

**Resources:**
- ImageOptim / Optimage - image optimization
- Stocksy - premium authentic stock images
- Squareshot - send product, receive pro photos
- Unsplash, Pixabay, Pexels - free stock
- IMGIX - image CDN with code-based manipulation

---

## #04 - Spice Up CTA Buttons
You're excited about your product - show it!

**Bland:** "Click Here" / "Sign Up"

**Actionable:**
- "Request a call from our agents"
- "Discover the wonders of science"
- "Unlock creativity for only $19"
- "Start my free trial"
- "Get instant access"

---

## #05 - When in Doubt, Double the Padding
Whitespace is breathing room for content AND your potential customer.

Digestible content improves focus and clarifies your offering.

**Action:** If your page feels overwhelming, double the padding. You'll be surprised.

**Resource:** Whitespace Inspiration - 1000+ landing pages with healthy whitespace at onepagelove.com

---

## #06 - Empathize with the Visitor's Problem
Start with their pain, then explain your solution simply.

**Before:**
"The world's most innovative invoice tracking software. XYZ Invoicing uses cutting-edge InvoAlgo algorithm to programmatically track unpaid invoices..."

**After:**
"Wasting time chasing late client payments? XYZ Invoicing sends automated reminders to clients with outstanding invoices."

**Resources:**
- Headlime - headline generator for brainstorming

---

## #07 - Avoid Center-Aligned or Justified Paragraphs
Both alignments cause reading fatigue on long paragraphs.

**Rule:** Any paragraph with 2+ lines should be left-aligned (or right-aligned for RTL languages).

**Resource:** "Rags, Widows & Orphans" - paragraph shapes to avoid

---

## #08 - Replace, Don't Add
8 testimonials don't need a 9th. Kick out the weakest.

- The 6 BEST examples of your work
- Your TOP 4 reviews
- The 3 STRONGEST case studies

**Goal:** Persuade with as little as possible.

---

## #09 - Set a Single Objective
One landing page = one goal.

**Wrong:** Sell eBook + promote job board + collect newsletter signups

**Right:** Sell eBook

The beauty of a landing page is the single canvas to persuade one action.

---

## #10 - Create a Text Color Hierarchy
Pure black (#000) on pure white (#FFF) is harsh and screams "amateur."

**Action:**
1. Use off-white background (#FAFAFA or similar)
2. Create grey hierarchy for text (headings darker, body lighter)
3. Check contrast meets accessibility standards

**Resources:**
- Contrast for Mac - ensure text meets accessibility standards
- Accessible Color Generator - find nearest passing color

---

## #11 - Remove Your Main Nav
If your landing page sits within a bigger website, hide the navigation.

Sending visitors to other pages is NOT your objective.

**Exception:** Sticky nav with page sections + CTA only (see #25)

---

## #12 - Use Footer for Pre-Sale Support
Visitor hasn't converted by the end of scroll = they still have doubts.

**Footer should include:**
- FAQs addressing pre-sale concerns
- Support email/contact
- Final CTA

This area could be the difference between visitor and customer.

---

## #13 - Highlight USP Among Feature List
12 features in a grid overwhelms first-time visitors.

**Action:**
1. Lead with your 2-3 Unique Selling Propositions
2. Emphasize benefits of choosing you over competition
3. Then show supporting features

---

## #14 - Offer More Pricing Tiers
Tier UP with more features, not DOWN by stripping core offering.

"Give people who would be happy to pay you more money, the opportunity to pay you more money." - Adam Wathan

**Resources:**
- Pricing Table Inspiration - 150+ examples at onepagelove.com
- "Pricing Psychology" - Help Scout's 10 strategies

---

## #15 - Use Specific Coupons
Track marketing channels with platform-specific coupon codes.

**Example for birthday sale:**
- Twitter10
- Insta10
- LinkedIn10
- NYTimes10

Dedicated coupons uncover which channels are effective while feeling exclusive.

---

## #16 - Embed Your Lead Forms
Every page load drops conversion rate.

**Wrong:** [Click here to Sign Up] â†’ loads new page

**Right:** (Enter your email) [Sign Up] embedded on page

Capture leads immediately.

---

## #17 - If You Market to Everyone, It Resonates with No One
Identify your audience. Research their language. Craft copy they appreciate.

**Generic:** "Join the biggest Lord of the Rings fan club online"

**Targeted:** "An online community where second breakfast is a dietary staple and Old Toby's smoke drifts from humble abodes."

**CTA:** "Enter Hobbiton for $9/month" vs "Join Club for $9/month"

What copy makes visitors think "These are my people"?

---

## #18 - Sit With Someone and Watch Them Convert
Two ways to experience horror:
1. Rent a gory R-rated film
2. Watch someone interact with your landing page

**Action:** Find someone from your target market. Watch them try to convert. Take notes on friction points.

---

## #19 - Set a Max-Width for Typography (CPL)
100% width paragraphs are difficult to read on large screens.

**Target:** 55-100 characters per line (CPL)

**Resources:**
- Tuning CPL article
- GRT Calculator - Golden Ratio Typography calculator

---

## #20 - Highlight Testimonial from Opinion Leader
Feature someone well-known to your target audience.

**Example scenario:** Selling icon pack. Visitor is unsure about $49 price. Scrolls to testimonial from Klaudia, designer at Spotify they follow on Twitter.

Thought process: "She has great taste. These icons must be best-in-class."

This is the game-changing Joker card in your deck.

---

## #21 - Avoid Animation Overkill
Gratuitous scroll transitions no longer impress.

Subtle animations are timeless and show intent.

**Rule:** Just because you can, doesn't mean you should.

---

## #22 - Consider a Color Scheme
A carefully crafted color scheme stands out and shows effort.

Visitors assume that effort carries over to your product/service.

**Resource:** Color Scheme Inspiration - 700+ colorful landing pages at onepagelove.com

---

## #23 - Avoid the Word "Cheap"
Positioning as "cheaper" sells your offering short and raises quality concerns.

**Use instead:**
- More affordable
- Economical
- Cost-effective
- Better value

---

## #24 - Create Haste
Offer discount or gift within diminishing timeframe.

**Elements:**
- Countdown timer positioned near CTA
- Clear savings amount ("Save 60%")
- Specific deadline

**Resource:** Countdown timer scripts

---

## #25 - Integrate Sticky Header Navigation
For long landing pages, sticky header provides:
- Easy section navigation
- CTA button always visible
- Reduced scroll fatigue

Keep it minimal: section links + primary CTA only.
