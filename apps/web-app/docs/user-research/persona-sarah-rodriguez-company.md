## 2. The Problem (Ordinary World)

### Current Situation
Sarah runs talent acquisition for NextGen Financial, a fast-growing fintech. The company needs to build an analytics division from scratch - the CEO wants a 6-person data science team operational within 90 days. Board is watching.

Sarah has budget approval. She has recruiter bandwidth. What she doesn't have is time to hire 6 individuals, hope they gel, and wait 12 months for them to become productive.

### What's Not Working
- **Individual hiring is slow**: Average time-to-fill for senior data scientist: 87 days. She needs 6.
- **Team formation is slower**: Tuckman says forming ‚Üí storming ‚Üí norming ‚Üí performing takes 6-12 months
- **Stars don't shine alone**: Last year's "rockstar" ML hire from Google? Decent, but keeps saying "at Google we did it this way"
- **Attrition compounds risk**: If 1 of 6 leaves in year 1, team chemistry resets to zero
- **CEO pressure**: "I hired you to solve this. Don't tell me about Tuckman's model."

### The Numbers That Haunt Sarah
| Stat | Source | Impact |
|------|--------|--------|
| 75% of employers hired wrong person | SHRM | "I've been part of that 75%" |
| $240K average cost of bad hire | SHRM | "That's 3 bad hires = $720K burned" |
| 46% performance decline when stars move alone | Groysberg | "My star hires aren't performing like their resumes promised" |
| 12 months to peak productivity | Gallup | "I don't have 12 months. I have 90 days." |

### Failed Alternatives
1. **Agency recruiters**: Expensive ($30K per hire), still 60-90 day timelines, no team chemistry guarantee
2. **Internal mobility**: Asked other divisions for transfers. Got 2 analysts, neither with ML experience.
3. **Acquihire negotiation**: Explored buying a 4-person startup. Took 4 months, fell apart on valuation.
4. **Contractor team**: Hired 3 contractors through Toptal. Good work, but they're leaving in 6 months.

### Emotional State
- **Pressured**: "The board presentation is in 90 days. I need bodies."
- **Skeptical**: "Every recruiter promises they can fill these roles. None deliver."
- **Frustrated**: "I keep hiring individuals who looked great on paper and can't work together."
- **Anxious**: "If I fail at this, it's my job on the line."

### The Core Tension
> "I can find talented individuals. But I can't manufacture chemistry. You can't interview for trust."

## 6. Transformation (The Ordeal)

### Key Decision Points

**Decision 1: Pitch it internally**
Sarah had to convince CEO, CFO, and General Counsel that team-based hiring wasn't crazy. She prepared a 10-slide deck with Groysberg research, law firm examples, and ROI projections.

**Decision 2: First expression of interest**
After 2 weeks of browsing, Sarah sent her first EOI to Alex Chen's team. Nerve-wracking: what if they said no?

**Decision 3: Negotiate as a package**
Finance wanted to negotiate individually with each team member. Sarah pushed back: "We're buying a team, not 4 individuals. One package, one deal."

**Decision 4: Fast-track the process**
Standard NextGen interview process: 5 rounds over 6 weeks. Sarah got approval for a compressed 2-week process for team hires.

### Moments of Doubt
- **Week 1**: Only 3 teams matched her criteria. "Is the supply side too thin?"
- **Week 3**: TechFlow team's salary ask was 15% above budget. "Are we getting played?"
- **Week 4**: One team member (Marcus) had a GitHub controversy from 2019. "Is this team actually vetted?"
- **Week 6**: Legal review took 3 weeks. CEO asked if she'd "given up on the unconventional approach."

### What Kept Her Going
1. **CEO buy-in**: He'd publicly endorsed the approach. Backing out would be visible failure.
2. **Early signal**: TechFlow team's response was enthusiastic and professional
3. **Comparative math**: 6 individual hires would take 6-8 months. This could close in 8 weeks.
4. **Gut instinct**: The TechFlow team's cohesion was visible in how they communicated

### Breakthroughs
- **Joint interview**: All 4 TechFlow members interviewed together. Chemistry was undeniable.
- **Reference check**: 3 references independently called them "the best team I've worked with"
- **Offer acceptance**: All 4 signed within 48 hours. No negotiation drama.
- **Day 15**: Team delivered first analytics dashboard. CEO texted Sarah: "Best hire we've made."

**Outcome**: Team hits the ground running. First deliverable in 60 days. CEO showcases the hire at all-hands. Sarah promoted, becomes internal champion for liftout strategy. NextGen acquires 2 more teams in the next year using the playbook she built.

**Probability**: 30%

**Why it could happen**:

### Worst Case Scenario ‚ùå
**Outcome**: Team arrives, but one member (Marcus) doesn't integrate well culturally. Creates friction with existing engineering team. After 6 months, he leaves. Team chemistry fractures. Two others follow within a year. $1.6M spent, division still understaffed.

**Probability**: 10%

**Why it's unlikely**:

### Most Likely Scenario üìä

- Timelines usually slip somewhat

| Risk | Mitigation |
