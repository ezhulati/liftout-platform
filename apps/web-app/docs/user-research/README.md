# Liftout User Research

Comprehensive user studies for Liftout's two primary user types, documented using the Hero's Journey framework and Jobs-to-be-Done methodology.

---

## User Types

### 1. Team Users (Individuals/Team Leads)
High-performing professionals who want to move to a new opportunity **together with their team**, preserving the chemistry, trust, and relationships they've built over years.

**Representative Persona:** [Alex Chen](./persona-alex-chen-team-lead.md) - Senior Data Scientist & Team Lead

### 2. Company Users (Hiring Organizations)
Companies seeking to acquire **intact teams** with proven track records, skipping the 12-month team formation phase and gaining day-one productivity.

**Representative Persona:** [Sarah Rodriguez](./persona-sarah-rodriguez-company.md) - VP of Talent Acquisition

## Research Foundation

These personas are grounded in:

### Academic Research
- **Groysberg "Chasing Stars"** (Harvard/Princeton, 2010): Star performers decline 46% when moving alone due to loss of firm-specific relational capital
- **SHRM Data**: 75% of employers admit to hiring the wrong person; average bad hire costs $240K
- **Gallup Research**: New hires take 12 months to reach peak productivity
- **Google Project Aristotle**: Psychological safety (built over years) accounts for 43% of team performance variance

### Industry Trends (2024-2025)
- Group lateral moves in law firms up 14% YoY
- Major acquihires: Microsoft/Inflection AI ($650M), Google/Character.AI ($2.7B)
- Team-based hiring becoming mainstream beyond traditional industries (law, banking, consulting)

## Target Industries

Liftouts are most common in industries where:
- Relationships drive value
- Teams have portable expertise
- Trust is mission-critical

| Industry | Example Liftout Pattern |
|----------|------------------------|
| **Law Firms** | Practice groups moving with client relationships |
| **Investment Banking** | Deal teams with sector expertise |
| **Consulting** | Practice leaders with methodology + clients |
| **Technology** | Engineering teams with domain expertise |
| **Healthcare** | Research labs, surgical teams |
| **Private Equity** | Deal teams with network + track record |

## How to Use This Research

### For Product Development
- Reference personas when designing features
- Validate user flows against documented journeys
- Prioritize based on Jobs to be Done

### For Marketing
- Use messaging frameworks from GTM doc
- Target channels identified in acquisition strategy
- Reference objections and how personas overcome them

### For Sales
- Understand buyer motivations and fears
- Address objections with documented counterarguments
- Speak to emotional and social jobs, not just functional
